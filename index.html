Biography & Education

Mitchell Schwarzer is a scholar, author, and educator whose research addresses the intersection of architectural design, city planning, infrastructure, and visual media. Over his distinguished career, he has documented and critiqued the evolving landscapes of the American West, with a strong focus on the San Francisco Bay Area.

As a city planner in the 1980s, he authored historic preservation elements of San Francisco's master plan andDowntown preservation statutes, providing him with direct, hands-on insight into urban development dynamics before transitioning to academia in the 1990s.
